The bread and butter

The four things people consistently use Glean for

To your point about not going too broad, these are the core, hard-to-replicate use cases. Each depends on the permissioned context layer, the part a generic LLM can't match without heavy token cost.

1

Trusted enterprise search across every permissioned system

Finding the right document, answer, or expert across SharePoint, Teams, CRM, ticketing, and internal knowledge, grounded in each person's access and without burning tokens on full-index scans.

2

Support and service resolution

Faster case handling by pulling answers across tickets, docs, and prior issues. Wealthsimple and Confluent both use it here, with Confluent reporting 5 to 10 minutes saved per ticket and Forrester citing about 20% fewer internal support requests.

3

Sales and account productivity

Meeting prep, account research, and follow-up drawing on prior calls and internal account context. TIME uses it for meeting prep and drafting initial pitches, and Confluent AEs pull up past deal cycles faster.

4

Cross-team and cross-geography knowledge reuse

Exactly the France-to-US example you raised. Surfacing what one region or team already solved so another can benefit, at scale, speed, and cost, which is hard to do manually in a matrixed org.

Once that context layer is in place, agents and workflow automation sit on top, so teams build repeatable workflows that retrieve context, reason across it, and take action.

The secret sauce

Why context is the hard part

To Prakash's point about the context layer, this is the piece that decides whether AI answers are trustworthy, and it's the part that's genuinely hard to build and keep current. It's also why the build-versus-buy math tends to favor buy.

01Accuracy & trust. Answers are only as good as retrieval. Context grounds every model in the right, permissioned source, so you get the canonical answer instead of a plausible guess.
02Permissions & security. Context has to respect who can see what across every system, or it becomes a data risk rather than an asset.
03Connectivity that keeps up. Connectors to structured and unstructured systems, maintained as tools, apps, and models keep changing.
04Pace of change. It's a full-time problem. Glean has 1,500+ people and hundreds of engineers focused only on this, and teams that went down the build path have come back.
Build the context once, then point any model or front end at it. That is why the same layer serves OpenAI, Copilot, or open-source models without re-wiring for each.

Does building context add token overhead per query?
No. The index and knowledge graph are built once, not re-sent on every query. Retrieval selects only the most relevant context to pass to the model, so per-query token use stays low, and Waldo optimizes model routing on top of that.

Why Glean when you're already invested in OpenAI and Microsoft?
Glean isn't a competing front end. It's the context layer underneath. You keep OpenAI, Copilot, or any model, and Glean grounds all of them in one permissioned index, knowledge graph, and retrieval layer, so you build that context once instead of re-wiring it for each tool.
